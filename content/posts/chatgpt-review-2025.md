# ChatGPT Review 2025: Is It Still the Best AI Chatbot?

**Monthly searches: ~200,000** — ChatGPT launched the AI era. Three years later, it's still the most-used AI assistant in the world. But is it still the best? We put GPT-4o through its paces to find out.

## What Is ChatGPT?

ChatGPT is OpenAI's conversational AI assistant, first released in November 2022. The current flagship model is **GPT-4o** (available to Plus and Pro subscribers), with GPT-4o mini available on the free tier.

As of 2025, ChatGPT has over 200 million weekly active users — making it the dominant AI assistant by usage.

## What's New in 2025

- **GPT-4o with memory** — remembers your preferences, projects, and style across all conversations
- **Advanced Voice Mode** — real-time voice conversations with natural turn-taking
- **Canvas** — collaborative writing and coding workspace
- **Sora integration** — generate videos from text prompts (Pro plan)
- **Operator agents** — automate tasks across web browsers

## Performance: What ChatGPT Does Best

### Writing
GPT-4o is excellent at long-form writing, marketing copy, and creative fiction. The output is polished, natural, and adapts well to tone instructions. Canvas mode makes iterative editing much smoother than the standard chat interface.

**Rating: 9/10**

### Coding
ChatGPT is one of the best AI coding assistants available. GPT-4o writes clean code across Python, JavaScript, TypeScript, SQL, and more. It explains its reasoning and handles debugging well. GitHub Copilot (also GPT-4 based) outperforms it in IDE integration, but for general coding tasks ChatGPT is excellent.

**Rating: 9/10**

### Reasoning & Analysis
GPT-4o handles multi-step logical problems well. The o1 and o3 models (available on Plus/Pro) are specifically optimized for hard math, science, and coding problems — and produce state-of-the-art results on reasoning benchmarks.

**Rating: 9/10** (o1/o3 models: 10/10)

### Research
ChatGPT's web search (powered by Bing) is useful but inconsistent. It hallucinates less than it used to, but still invents citations occasionally. For research requiring citations, Perplexity AI is more reliable.

**Rating: 7/10**

## ChatGPT Strengths

✅ **Largest ecosystem** — integrates with 1000+ apps via GPTs and API
✅ **Most versatile** — writing, coding, analysis, image generation, voice, video
✅ **Best mobile app** — polished iOS and Android experience
✅ **Advanced reasoning** — o1/o3 models for complex math and science
✅ **Memory** — remembers context across sessions
✅ **Plugin/GPT marketplace** — thousands of specialized agents

## ChatGPT Weaknesses

❌ **Hallucination rate** — still makes up facts, especially for recent events
❌ **Context window** — 128K tokens (Claude's 200K is larger)
❌ **Free tier limitations** — rate limited and uses weaker model
❌ **Price** — $200/month for Pro is expensive
❌ **Privacy** — conversations used for training by default (can opt out)

## Who Should Use ChatGPT?

**Best for:**
- Professionals who need an all-purpose AI assistant
- Developers building apps on GPT API
- Anyone who wants the largest feature set in one tool
- Complex reasoning tasks (o1/o3 models)

**Consider alternatives if:**
- You write very long documents (Claude's 200K context is better)
- You need reliable citation-backed research (try Perplexity)
- You're deeply in the Google ecosystem (Gemini integrates with Docs/Gmail)

## Verdict

**Score: 9/10**

ChatGPT remains the best all-around AI assistant in 2025. The breadth of features — writing, coding, voice, images, video, memory, agents — is unmatched by any single competitor. The free tier is genuinely useful, and the Plus plan at $20/month is excellent value for daily users.

The main weaknesses are hallucination (improving but still present) and context window size (Claude wins on very long documents). But for most use cases, ChatGPT is the right choice.

**Start with the free tier** to evaluate it for your specific needs, then upgrade to Plus if you hit limits.
